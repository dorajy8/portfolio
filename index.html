Recurrent Spiking Neural Network Research

Neuroscience Research Project

Under the lead of Prof. Yuqing Zhu, I worked with four other students during summer 2024 to research RSNN in terms of its potential to complete tasks and its functional similarities with how our brain carries out information processing.

We found that compared to backpropagation, using evolutionary algorithms to train RSNN pushes the recurrent layer of the network to perform brain-like behavior, which represents the desired brain activities that neuroscientists want to observe. Antara and I presented our results at the Society of Neuroscience conference in Chicago 2024.
